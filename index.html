Survey on: Formalisms for Robotic Mission Specification and Execution: A Comparative Analysis

Introduction

We are investigating how different modeling formalisms, Behavior Trees (BTs), State Machines (SMs), Hierarchical Task Networks (HTNs), and the Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN), support the specification and execution of robotic missions.

This survey aims to validate the results of our study by gathering expert feedback on the correctness, completeness, and applicability of our findings. In particular, we ask you to review a subset of results related to the formalism(s) in which you have expertise and to evaluate whether our characterization, examples, and identified capabilities accurately reflect current practice in robotics.

Your participation is voluntary, and your responses will be processed anonymously, stored securely, and used solely for academic research purposes. Only the research team will have access to your contact data. You may withdraw at any time by closing the survey before submission.

If you have any questions or concerns about the study, please contact us at:

RQ1 - Modeling of Control Structures and Mission Concepts

This research question analyzes how each formalism supports the specification of fundamental control structures which govern the flow of a robotic mission. In addition, RQ1 investigates the ability of the formalisms to express key mission concepts.

Mission Concepts

Skill: a basic robot capability (e.g., grasp) implemented at a low level and exposed as a reusable action.
Task: a higher-level behavior composed of one or more skills, describing what the robot should accomplish.
Data: information used or produced during execution and shared across tasks or components.
Communication: the exchange of information between the robot and external entities (humans, robots, systems).
Events: occurrences that may trigger or influence the mission flow.
Errors: undesired outcomes during execution.
Pre-/Post-conditions: requirements that must hold before starting a task and the expected state after completing it.

RQ3 - Tools Analysis

This research question investigates the ecosystem of tools supporting each formalism. The analysis examines whether tools provide modeling, execution, monitoring, debugging, or planning functionalities.

Modeling refers to graphical or structured interfaces for creating and visualizing mission specifications.
Execution denotes a tool’s ability to interpret and run a model, either standalone or within ROS.
Monitoring concerns the provision of real-time information on mission status during execution.
Debugging supports diagnosing undesired behaviors through trace visualization, state inspection, and event logging.
Planning refers to the automatic generation of task decompositions or action sequences.
