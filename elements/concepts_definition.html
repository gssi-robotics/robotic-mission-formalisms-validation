• Skill: programmed action that represents a basic capability of the robot. It is implemented at a lower level and exposed as a reusable action for the mission specification.
  • Task: symbolic representation of a higher-level robotic behavior realized as a combination of skills. Tasks specify what must be done and abstracts from the concrete implementation
  • Data: information used or produced during execution and shared across tasks or components to parametrize skills/tasks.
  • Communication: the exchange of information between the robot and external entities (humans, robots, systems).
  • Events: occurrences that may trigger or influence the mission flow.
  • Errors: events that arise from faults, failures, or any unexpected conditions preventing the mission from being executed without proper handling.
  • Pre-/Post-conditions: formalize the states of the system before and after the execution of a task or skill.
